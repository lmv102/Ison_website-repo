    • Glasses that convert physical text to audio

    Ison

    People with visual impairments struggle to read physical text, like restaurant menus and billboards, which affects their quality of life. Ison aids with that struggle by providing our users with more independence. Ison is a pair of smart reading glasses that reads text in the world and converts text-to-speech for the user to listen. Similar products are already on the market, but they stand out when worn, and some even require subscriptions to unlock features. Unlike the competition, Ison offers discreet reading assistance with extra features, like translation and a long battery life, for only a one-time payment.

    Dive into

    Ison's Overview


    Look into each of the

    Subsystems

    Power

    The power supply setup of Ison glasses is pivotal to its functionality. A rechargeable LiPo battery powers Ison’s chosen modules. Charging a 3.7-V lithium battery to 5 V typically involves utilizing a charging module.

    Text-to-Speech

    This subsystem consists of hardware and software to accomplish the text-to-speech conversion task. The hardware component is the camera. The software components are the AI model for optical character recognition (OCR) and the text-to-speech algorithm.

    Bluetooth

    This subsystem consists of the project's Bluetooth capabilities. It transmits the picture data and receives the audio output file from the user’s smartphone.

    Companion App

    Ison has a companion app that works as a processor while featuring language selection, volume settings options, and bluetooth setup.

    Audio Output

    To output audio for the users of Ison glasses, the team chose bone conduction transducers.
